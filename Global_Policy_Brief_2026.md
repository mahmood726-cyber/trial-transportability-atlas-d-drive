# Global Policy Brief: Bridging the Transportability Gap (2026)

## Overview
This brief summarizes the findings of the **Trial Transportability Atlas** simulation phase. We evaluated the impact of strategic policy interventions on the readiness of the African region to transport cardiovascular trial evidence (SGLT2 inhibitors and GLP-1 agonists).

## Key Findings: The African Bottleneck
- **Baseline Readiness**: Africa currently operates at a Transportability Index of ~0.43, well below the viability threshold for complex trial protocols.
- **The Workforce Lever**: Doubling physician density provides a **+33% lift** in transportability index, compared to only **+12%** for significant GDP growth.
- **System Viability**: Regional viability (>0.55) is only achieved through clinical workforce hardening or integrated health reforms.

## Strategic Recommendations
1.  **Workforce-First Investment**: Prioritize clinical capacity building over general economic uplift for immediate transportability gains.
2.  **Integrated Reform**: Combine a 1.8x workforce boost with a 1.5x increase in health spending to reach a "High Viability" index of 0.63.
3.  **Transportability-Centric Design**: Future cardiovascular trials in Africa must be simplified to match current clinical workforce densities unless system hardening occurs.
